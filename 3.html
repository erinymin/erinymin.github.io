good job!

The phrase carved on the stamp is a phrase used on a Korean traditional stamp that people use a lot at school. The phrase directly translates to “Good Job!”. It is an iconic stamp that evokes good feelings and nostalgia. It is also a childhood memory for a lot of Korean children, including me. I created my variation on the stamp.

There are usually a boy and a girl, but instead, I carved two women to represent my color and art style. By utilizing a stamping technique to print these prints. These variations of the print were used in the common school pen colors commonly used during school.

Being able to personalize my idea of my childhood through my interpretations can hopefully reach out to the viewers more. It is personalized to my own culture and intending for it to be a creative style that represents me in the present moment.
